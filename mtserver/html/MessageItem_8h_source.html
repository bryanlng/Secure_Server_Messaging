1 /*
2  Object to hold all the metadata of the message, as well as the message itself.
3 
4  After parsing incoming messages, the server stuffs all the data into a
5  MessageItem, then puts it onto a queue (wqueue) similar to the queue used to
6  manage WorkItems.
7 
8  MessageItem objects will only exist on the server-side. Incoming messages from
9  clients as well as outgoing messages to clients will be in the form of strings.
10 
11  Format of message:
12  Case 1: Regular message
13  -timestamp <delimiter> date_formatted <delimiter> message <delimiter> sender <delimiter>
14  -thread_id is ignored, as that field is taken from the thread itself
15  -Delimiter: :::::::
16  -time_of_last_received = -1
17  -Constructor 1 takes care of this
18 
19  Case 2: Timestamp message
20  -timestamp <special timestamp delimiter>
21  -Delimiter: ::Timestamp::
22  -time_of_last_received != -1
23  -timestamp = -1
24  -All string fields are ""
25  -Constructor 2 takes care of this
26 
27  Constructor 3 is used in the process of sending messages from the master log
28  to the client.
29 
30  Timestamp of last message received:
31  -special message sent by client
32  -If it's just a regular message, time_of_last_received = -1
33  -Else, time_of_last_received != -1
34 */
